## LinkedIn

**Character count: 1,048/1,300**

Samsung engineers pasted proprietary source code into ChatGPT three times in 20 days. The data became irrecoverable.

That's the thing with LLM features in production — every user message is a potential PII leak. To the provider, your logs, your vector store. Cyberhaven Labs analyzed 1.6M workers and reported 11% of pasted data was confidential. Traditional DLP tools missed most of it.

The tempting fix is regex patterns for SSNs and credit cards. That gets about 0.65 recall — 35% of PII slips through. Names, addresses, medical conditions? Invisible to regex. A hybrid approach (regex + NER) pushes recall to 0.85–0.95, and it runs synchronously before the LLM ever sees the data.

Detection overhead: 0.07ms at p50. Additional cost at 100K requests/day: $0.10. Basically pays for itself the moment it prevents a single compliance inquiry.

Full pattern with TS + Python implementations, benchmarks, and runbook: https://github.com/kchia/production-llm-patterns/tree/main/patterns/safety/pii-detection
